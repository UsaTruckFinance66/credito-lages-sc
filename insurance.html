Commercial Truck Insurance Guide

In the United States, trucking insurance is more than a legal requirement; it's the backbone of your business security. For 2024, owner-operators must navigate rising premiums by choosing the right coverage mix.

Primary Liability

This is mandated by federal regulations. Minimum coverage is usually $750,000, but most shippers require $1,000,000 to move their freight.

Cargo & Physical Damage

Protecting the load is vital. Cargo insurance typically covers up to $100,000. Physical Damage protects your personal investment—the truck itself—against accidents, theft, or fire.
